The Methodology

The museum is
the methodology.

Museum Planning LLC does not sell hours. The firm sells a methodology — thirty years of codified expertise in how museums get built, how they get funded, how they get used, and how they last.

Every engagement begins with the same question: what does this community need that it does not yet have? The answer shapes everything that follows — the feasibility study, the strategic plan, the master plan, the exhibition design. The process is the same whether the institution is a $300,000 community history museum in Georgia or a $25 million science center in Indonesia.

The methodology is documented in two books published by Bloomsbury, embodied in a suite of planning tools used by museum professionals worldwide, and tested across 40+ institutions on three continents.

The Theoretical Foundation

Museums will no longer be defined as buildings — but as distributed interpretive systems.

In November 2025, Museum Planning LLC published the Convergence Era white paper — a framework for understanding the transformation now underway at the intersection of industrial design, artificial intelligence, spatial computing, and cultural institutions.

The museum is the proof of concept. Every planning tool, every MVI report, every strategic plan we deliver is built inside this framework. The institution you build in 2026 will still be operating in 2056. It needs to be ready for what comes next.
