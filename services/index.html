Museum Planning Services:
From Vision to Opening Day

Museum Planning LLC guides cultural institutions through every stage of development — from the first community assessment through post-opening evaluation. Our four-phase process has shaped more than 40 museums worldwide over 25 years of practice.

01
Phase One

Institutional Planning

Before a single line is drawn, museums need a clear strategic foundation. Institutional planning establishes why the museum exists, who it serves, and whether it can operate sustainably. This phase produces the critical documents that boards, donors, and civic partners need before committing capital.

  • Community Assessment — audience research and needs analysis
  • Mission, Vision & Values — organizational identity framework
  • Feasibility Study — attendance projections, staffing, and operating cost modeling
  • Strategic Planning — multi-year roadmap from planning through opening
02
Phase Two

Facility Planning

Facility planning translates institutional goals into physical space. This phase produces the blueprints — programmatic, experiential, and architectural — that govern every construction and design decision. It also structures the capital campaign required to fund the building and its exhibitions.

  • Museum Master Plan — building program, floor plans, phasing, and budget
  • Capital Campaign Plan — fundraising strategy, donor pipeline, naming opportunities
  • Visitor Experience Plan — interpretive framework for every guest touchpoint
  • Exhibition Design — concept through fabrication-ready CAD documentation
  • Building Design — architectural programming and systems coordination
03
Phase Three

Operational Planning

As construction progresses, the institution must be ready to operate on opening day. Operational planning builds the systems, plans, and infrastructure that keep a museum running — financially, programmatically, and physically. Personnel typically accounts for 60% of a museum's annual budget, making staffing modeling essential.

  • Disaster Preparedness & Emergency Response Plan
  • Public Programming — tours, events, education, and community initiatives
  • Business Plan — operating cost modeling and revenue diversification strategy
  • Marketing Plan — brand identity, digital strategy, and audience development
  • Building Construction — owner's representation through the construction phase
  • Exhibition Fabrication — oversight of interactives, AV, graphics, and installation
04
Phase Four

Opening & Operations

A successful opening is planned, not improvised. The final phase covers grand opening strategy, early-stage community engagement, and the formal evaluation cycles that determine what's working and what must evolve. Post-opening assessment closes the loop and initiates the next strategic planning cycle.

  • Museum Opening — press previews, VIP events, ribbon-cutting coordination
  • Community Impact — measuring cultural, educational, and economic contributions
  • Evaluation — exhibition effectiveness assessment against learning objectives
  • Institutional Assessment — full operational review to launch the next planning cycle
